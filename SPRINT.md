# Sprint 17 — Milestone 12: Agent-addressable review — prose-pass slice

This Sprint migrates the **prose-pass family** onto the shared review contract M10 landed, the third of the four one-milestone-per-sprint slices (M10–M13 in ROADMAP.md; the shared design lives in the completed M10 section there). It follows the same AI-plus-human review directive that reshaped M11 (recorded in the M11 Notes): the `amanuensis-review` companion captures and writes, the human decides, and conventions that existed only to make human hand-editing of markdown cheap are retired where retiring them makes agent review simpler, more straightforward, or more reliable. Concretely: `prose_pass` emits structured review items — a `<!-- review-id: ... -->` anchor plus blank `- Decision:` / `- Decision-note:` fields on **every finding, `KEEP` included** — replacing the positional `Annotation:` line and its bracketed token-set placeholder; `prose_fix` consumes pure per-unit decisions through the shared validator, exactly like `compliance_fix`; and the `prose_pass` family flips to `adopted` in `agents/review-grammars.yaml` with its container shape settled (`container_pattern: "#### Findings"`) so a pre-M12 report can never validate as proceed. `prose_pass` declares **no `fanout_categories`**: the pass is deliberately selective (5–10 findings), so per-entry decisions are the point — the locked M5 no-bulk convention, restated in the post-Sprint-16 vocabulary. Big-bang, as with compliance and anti-AI: after this Sprint the old positional prose-pass annotation format is an invalid input to `prose_fix` and no compatibility path is kept.

The one hand-edit affordance retired here is **anchorless `KEEP`**. Before this Sprint, a `KEEP` finding carried no anchor and no fields, so `prose_fix` auto-treated it as `SKIP` and the human never touched it — the exact "accepted and unreviewed look identical at the review-unit level" ambiguity shared-design principle 4 exists to kill. Under the directive, `KEEP` becomes a first-class review unit: every finding is anchored, the human decides every one (a `KEEP` finding's typical decision is `SKIP`, confirming the producer's keep), and the reviewer's `KEEP/TIGHTEN/FLATTEN/REWRITE` rubric is otherwise unchanged. Making `KEEP` a unit also **dissolves the all-`KEEP` container problem** the ROADMAP flagged, so `scripts/validate-review-artifact.sh` ships **byte-for-byte unchanged** — verified at planning (see Background).

## Background — what is and isn't wrong today

Established by inspection during planning; tasks should not re-derive this.

- **★ The owner directive that settled the container question.** The ROADMAP M12 Notes flagged an "all-`KEEP` container problem": `KEEP` findings carried no anchors, so a legitimately all-`KEEP` `#### Findings` section had zero anchored units — indistinguishable, to a plain container check, from a pre-M12 unanchored report — and the candidate fix was a body sentinel plus a validator change. The owner resolved it differently at Sprint 17 planning: *make `KEEP` items annotated review units like every other finding.* The anchorless-`KEEP` shortcut existed only to spare the human deciding on lines the producer already liked; that is a hand-edit ergonomics convention, and under AI-plus-human review it is exactly the accepted-vs-unreviewed gap the milestone closes. With every finding anchored, the container problem disappears with the stock validator (next bullet).
- **The prose_pass grammar block already exists but is `pending`.** `agents/review-grammars.yaml:117-146` defines the family from M10.1: `item_line_pattern: "^##### "` (the finding's `##### [short label]` heading is its item line, anchor immediately above it), `id_item_pattern: "finding-[0-9][0-9]"`, `id_min_locations: 0` (chapter-scoped, no scene segment), `tokens: FIX SKIP ESCALATE`, `payload_optional: FIX`. It carries **no `container_pattern`** and is marked `adoption: pending` / `migrates_in: M12`. Its `item_shape`, `blank_means`, `counting`, and `review_id_item` still describe the old "KEEP findings are not units / actionable findings only" model and must be rewritten (Task 1). Container settlement is the only structural gap; every other machine-read key is already correct and stays.
- **The container mechanism, verified at planning.** `container_pattern` (`scripts/validate-review-artifact.sh:266-276`, `:424-448`) flags a heading section that holds zero anchored units before the next heading (of any level) as invalid — a positional/pre-migration report. Two facts govern prose_pass: (1) the finding item line is itself a heading (`##### `), and the validator's special case at `:425` (`NR != anchor_line + 1`) keeps an anchor-then-`#####` unit open — but `close_container()` at `:429` still fires on every heading, so a container closes at the first `#####` sub-heading, and an *anchorless* `#####` heading inside `#### Findings` closes the container at zero units (a false invalid). Making every finding anchored removes every anchorless `#####` heading from `#### Findings`, so this never fires. (2) prose_pass's report carries sibling headings that legitimately hold no units — `#### Top priorities`, `### Chapter-level diagnosis` and its `#### ` subsections, `#### Lines worth preserving` — so `container_pattern` must be the **single literal `#### Findings` heading**, not a bare `#### ` (which would wrongly demand units under `#### Top priorities` etc.). The `index($0, container_pattern) == 1` match at `:427` means `#### Findings` matches only a heading starting with exactly `#### Findings`; `#### Top priorities`, `#### What the prose…`, `### Chapter-level diagnosis` do not match. This is ROADMAP M10's "container settlement must accommodate headings that legitimately hold no anchored units," and it is why prose_pass's container is one specific heading, unlike anti_ai where every `### ` is a category.
- **Verified with the stock validator and a scratch grammar** (prose_pass `adopted`, `container_pattern: "#### Findings"`, `container_exempt_suffix: "— none"`, no other change): a mixed report (a `KEEP`/`SKIP` unit, a `TIGHTEN`/`FIX` unit, a `FLATTEN`/blank unit) yields total 3, decided 1, skipped 1, pending 1, exit 4; an all-`KEEP` report with every unit decided `SKIP` yields exit 0 (proceed); a zero-findings report headed `#### Findings — none` yields total 0, exit 0; and a pre-M12 report (unanchored `##### ` findings under `#### Findings`) fails structural validation as an unanchored container, exit 3. `scripts/validate-review-artifact.sh` therefore ships **byte-for-byte unchanged**, as it did for M11.

## Conventions adopted by this Sprint

Locked at planning (the starred item is the owner decision from this Sprint's planning session); tasks don't rediscover them.

- **★ `KEEP` becomes an annotated review unit; anchorless `KEEP` is retired.** Every finding — `KEEP`, `TIGHTEN`, `FLATTEN`, `REWRITE` — carries a `review-id` anchor and blank `Decision:` / `Decision-note:` fields, and the human decides every one. A `KEEP` finding's typical decision is `SKIP` (confirming the producer's keep). Rationale: anchorless `KEEP` existed to spare the human deciding on lines the producer liked — a hand-edit ergonomics convention — and it was the last place a prose finding could be "accepted" (auto-`SKIP`) without looking any different from "unreviewed" (shared-design principle 4). Making `KEEP` a unit closes that gap **and** dissolves the all-`KEEP` container problem: with every finding anchored, an all-`KEEP` `#### Findings` section holds anchored units (valid), a pre-M12 report's unanchored `#### Findings` fails the container check (invalid), and the stock validator needs no change. Rejected alternatives: keeping `KEEP` anchorless and exempting the section with a body sentinel plus a validator change (touches the locked script for a case the anchoring already handles); dropping `KEEP` as a finding entirely (loses the reviewer's "I considered this line and it holds" signal and touches the severity rubric).
- **The container is the single literal `#### Findings` heading.** `container_pattern: "#### Findings"`, not a bare `#### `. Unlike anti_ai (where every `### ` heading is a category container), prose_pass's report carries sibling headings that legitimately hold no units — `#### Top priorities`, `### Chapter-level diagnosis` and its `#### ` subsections, `#### Lines worth preserving` — and the `index()==1` prefix match means `#### Findings` matches only the literal Findings heading, leaving those siblings alone. This is ROADMAP M10's "container settlement must accommodate headings that legitimately hold no anchored units." The step and fixture must both emit the Findings heading as exactly `#### Findings`.
- **A report that found nothing records `#### Findings — none` plus `No findings.`** — the container-exempt heading (compliance's `— CLEAN` mechanism, via `container_exempt_suffix: "— none"`) plus one line, no unit. This is the rare all-clean case (no problems and no notable keeps), the analog of anti-AI's `No flags.` and compliance's `— CLEAN`. It is distinct from an all-`KEEP` report, which has anchored `KEEP` units.
- **No fan-out anywhere in this family.** prose_pass declares no `fanout_categories`; the pass is selective (5–10 findings) so per-entry decisions are the point (locked M5, restated). The companion offers no category grouping and no fan-out for prose_pass; `agents/review-validation.md`'s Decision-automation rules already forbid fan-out on a family with no declaration, so that file is untouched.
- **Blank means pending, for every finding, with no exceptions.** The report emits blank `Decision:` / `Decision-note:` for every finding; the human fills each. Any finding with a blank `Decision:` — `KEEP` findings included — is a pending unit and blocks `prose_fix` as `review_pending`, naming the pending review-ids (per-unit gate, compliance model). This replaces the old whole-file gate (`agents/steps/prose-fix.md:37`) and the `KEEP`-auto-`SKIP` shortcut.
- **Per-entry tokens are unchanged; their carrier changes.** `FIX` / `FIX: <instruction>` / `SKIP` / `ESCALATE` move from the positional `Annotation:` line into the `Decision:` field. Big-bang: after this Sprint the positional form is an invalid input to `prose_fix`. `Action:` (`KEEP/TIGHTEN/FLATTEN/REWRITE`) stays as the producer's severity recommendation, distinct from the human's `Decision:` — the fixer's bare-`FIX` behavior keys off `Action:` as it does today.
- **The `KEEP` severity case in the fixer.** A `KEEP` finding carries no severity fix strategy (the producer diagnosed no problem), so a bare `FIX` on it has no default and `prose_fix` treats it as `ESCALATE` (parallel to anti_ai's no-bare-`FIX` categories, `agents/steps/anti-ai-fix.md:81-88`); `FIX: <instruction>` is honored sentence-locally. The validator stays family-level and token-agnostic (it does not read `Action:`), so it accepts `FIX` on a `KEEP` unit; the fixer owns this fallback, exactly as anti_ai's fixer owns its category fallbacks.
- **The validator ships byte-for-byte unchanged.** Verified at planning against a scratch grammar: making every finding anchored plus the two container keys reproduces the mixed / all-`KEEP` / zero-findings / pre-M12 verdicts with the stock script. The counts and verdicts are the things that must never be wrong; the M10-locked script stays. `agents/review-validation.md` is likewise unchanged.
- **Artifact-state mechanics are untouched.** Freshness stays a derived predicate; override stays stale-axis-only, anchor-gated, recorded in the apply log with the existing `Override:` block grammar (`agents/steps/prose-fix.md:121-147`); `agents/orchestrator.md` and `agents/chapters.md` are not edited this Sprint.
